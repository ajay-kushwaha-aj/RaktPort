Scope & Applicability
Who this policy covers and how it applies

This Privacy Policy governs the collection, processing, storage, and disclosure of personal data by RaktPort (hereinafter "Platform," "we," "us," or "our") — operated under applicable Indian law, principally the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023 and rules notified thereunder.

This Policy applies to all users accessing RaktPort via web, mobile, or API — including voluntary donors, registered hospitals, blood banks, and administrative personnel.

Data Fiduciary: RaktPort acts as a Data Fiduciary under the DPDP Act 2023 and is responsible for determining the purposes and means of processing your personal data.

By registering or using our services, you acknowledge having read and understood this Policy and provide informed, free, specific, and unambiguous consent to process your personal data as described herein.

Data We Collect
Categories of personal data processed by RaktPort
  • Identity Data: Full name, date of birth, gender, government-issued ID (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport), ABHA Health ID.
  • Health & Medical Data: Blood group, haemoglobin levels, donation history, eligibility status, last donation date. This constitutes Sensitive Personal Data under DPDP Act.
  • Contact Data: Mobile number, email address, residential address, city/district/pincode.
  • Institutional Data (Hospitals/Blood Banks): Organisation name, CDSCO licence number, NHA registration, authorised personnel details.
  • Technical Data: Device identifiers, IP address, browser type, OS, session logs, access timestamps — processed for security and fraud prevention.
  • Transaction Data: Donation records, requisition IDs (RTIDs), transfusion tracking events, appointment logs.

No unnecessary collection: We adhere to the data minimisation principle. Only data necessary for our stated purposes is collected.

Legal Basis for Processing (DPDP Act 2023)
Why we are lawfully permitted to process your data
  • Consent (S.6 DPDP): For all non-essential data processing, we obtain your explicit, informed, and withdrawable consent via a layered notice mechanism at registration and at each material data-use event.
  • Legitimate Use (S.7 DPDP): Processing required to fulfil a service you explicitly requested — including matching donors to urgent hospital requisitions — constitutes a legitimate use under the Act.
  • Medical Emergency (S.7(e) DPDP): In life-threatening situations, contact and blood-group data may be shared with verified hospitals without prior consent, consistent with the Act's emergency exemptions.
  • Legal Obligation: We may process and retain data to comply with orders from courts, regulators, CERT-In, or other competent authorities under Indian law.
ABHA & Health Record Data
Handling of Ayushman Bharat Health Account data

RaktPort integrates with the Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM) Health Information Exchange framework. Linking your ABHA ID to RaktPort is voluntary and enables interoperability with your national health records.

All Health Information (HI) exchange via ABDM infrastructure uses purpose-limited, time-bound consent artefacts governed by the ABDM Consent Manager Framework (HIE-CM). No health data leaves the ABDM ecosystem without your explicit consent artefact.

  • Donation records may be pushed to your ABHA-linked Personal Health Record (PHR) if you opt in.
  • ABHA IDs are stored encrypted; no plain-text ABHA numbers appear in logs.
  • You may de-link your ABHA ID at any time without losing your RaktPort donor history.
How We Use Your Data
Specific, documented purposes of data processing
  • Match verified donors to urgent hospital blood requisitions in real-time. Blood group and location data are stored and processed strictly for matching donors to requisitions and are not used for any other purpose.
  • Send emergency blood need alerts, appointment reminders, and eligibility countdowns via SMS/email.
  • Generate anonymised aggregate analytics (national blood inventory insights) for public health planning — no individual is identifiable.
  • Fraud prevention: cross-verify donation intervals, detect duplicate accounts, and investigate misuse of RTID codes.
  • Maintain an auditable IEC 62304 / ISO 13485-compliant software lifecycle record — no additional personal data is collected for this purpose.
  • Respond to your support queries and grievances within the statutory timelines.

We will never use your data for advertising profiling, sale to third parties, or any purpose not listed above without obtaining fresh, specific consent.

Submit requests to: privacy@raktport.in — response within 30 days per DPDP Act timelines.

Data Security & Cybersecurity Framework
Technical and organisational measures protecting your data
  • Encryption at Rest: All sensitive fields (health data, Aadhaar references, ABHA IDs) are encrypted using AES-256-GCM.
  • Encryption in Transit: TLS 1.3 enforced on all endpoints; HSTS headers enabled; certificate pinning on mobile clients.
  • Access Control: Role-based access control (RBAC) with least-privilege principle; MFA enforced for all administrative accounts.
  • Penetration Testing: Documented third-party VAPT conducted at least annually; findings tracked to closure per OWASP guidelines.
  • Ransomware Protection: Immutable off-site backups, anomaly-based detection, incident response plan tested bi-annually.
  • CERT-In Compliance: Cyber incidents reported to CERT-In within 6 hours per the 2022 directive; affected Data Principals notified without undue delay.
  • Audit Logging: Tamper-evident audit trails retained for 5 years per IEC 62304 software lifecycle documentation requirements.
Data Retention
How long we keep different categories of data
  • Active donor records: Retained for the duration of your active account plus 7 years (medical record retention standard, MCI guidelines).
  • Donation transactions / RTIDs: Retained for 10 years per blood bank regulatory requirements (CDSCO, Drugs & Cosmetics Act).
  • Consent records: Retained for the lifetime of the account and 3 years post-deletion to demonstrate regulatory compliance.
  • Technical/log data: Rolling 180-day retention; anonymised aggregate statistics may be kept indefinitely.
  • Deleted accounts: Personal data is purged within 30 days of deletion request, subject to legal hold obligations.
Third-Party Disclosures
When and with whom we share your data

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal data. Disclosure occurs only in the following documented circumstances:

  • Verified Hospitals & Blood Banks: Minimum necessary donor contact + blood-group data shared only upon matched, time-bound requisition with documented consent.
  • Cloud & Infrastructure: Data stored on NHA-empanelled cloud providers operating ISO 27001-certified data centres within India (data residency maintained).
  • Communication Providers: Mobile/email OTP and alert delivery through TRAI-registered transactional SMS gateways and email services under data processing agreements.
  • Government & Regulators: Disclosed only when legally compelled by a valid court order, CERT-In directive, or regulatory authority — and notified to you where legally permissible.

All third parties are bound by Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) requiring DPDP Act compliance and prohibiting further disclosure.

Cookies & Tracking
How we use cookies and your controls
  • Strictly Necessary: Session tokens, CSRF tokens — cannot be disabled without breaking functionality.
  • Analytics (opt-in): Anonymised usage metrics via privacy-respecting analytics. Disabled by default; enabled only with your explicit consent.
  • No third-party advertising trackers — we do not use Google Ads, Meta Pixel, or similar commercial tracking tools.
Children's Privacy
Protection of minors under 18

RaktPort is strictly not intended for use by persons under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect data from minors. Blood donation eligibility begins at age 18 per Indian law.

If we identify that a minor has registered, all associated data will be permanently deleted within 48 hours and, if required, reported to relevant authorities per DPDP Act provisions on child data protection.
