2. Platform Purpose & Facilitation Role
What RaktPort is and what it is not

RaktPort is a digital facilitation platform. It connects voluntary blood donors, registered hospitals, and licensed blood banks through verified, real-time requests. RaktPort does not:

  • Engage in the commercial trade, sale, or purchase of human blood or blood components
  • Provide medical advice, diagnosis, or clinical treatment
  • Guarantee blood availability, donor acceptance, or transfusion outcomes
  • Replace or substitute professional medical supervision during donation or transfusion

All donations facilitated through RaktPort adhere to voluntary, non-remunerated donation principles as specified by the National Blood Policy of India and WHO guidelines.

3. User Eligibility
Who may register and use RaktPort
  • Age: You must be at least 18 years of age. Blood donation eligibility begins at 18 per Indian medical standards.
  • Donor Eligibility: Donors must meet all medical criteria including weight ≥50 kg, haemoglobin ≥12.5 g/dL, and the prescribed donation intervals (3 months for males, 4 months for females).
  • Institutional Registration: Hospitals must hold a valid CDSCO/State Drug Authority licence for blood bank operation. Blood banks must be NHA-empanelled or state-registered.
  • Accurate Information: All data submitted at registration must be truthful, current, and verifiable. Submission of false information is grounds for immediate account termination.
  • Legal Capacity: You must have the legal capacity to enter into binding agreements under Indian Contract Act, 1872.

6. ABDM Integration Terms
Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission obligations

RaktPort's integration with the Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM) ecosystem is governed by NHA's Health Information Exchange (HIE) framework and the ABDM participation agreement.

  • Linking your ABHA ID is voluntary. You may unlink at any time without loss of donation history.
  • Health information exchange via ABDM occurs only with your active, time-bound consent artefact via the HIE-CM (Health Information Exchange and Consent Manager) protocol.
  • RaktPort stores ABHA identifiers in encrypted form only and does not retain ABHA-linked health records outside your explicit consent scope.
  • Hospitals accessing donor health information via ABDM-linked flows must be NHA-registered Health Information Users (HIUs) with valid credentials.

8. RTID System & Traceability
RaktPort Tracking ID — the backbone of our audit trail

The RaktPort Tracking ID (RTID) is a system-generated, Luhn-36 checksum-validated 19-character unique identifier assigned to every donation and hospital requisition event.

  • Each RTID embeds type, date, and a cryptographically random component. RTIDs are non-transferable and tied to the specific event record.
  • RTIDs support end-to-end blood traceability from donor to recipient — a key requirement under CDSCO blood bank regulations and IEC 62304 audit obligations.
  • Forgery, duplication, or misuse of RTIDs is a criminal offence and grounds for immediate platform ban and law enforcement referral.
